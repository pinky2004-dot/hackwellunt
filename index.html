Project Topics

Choose from 12 pre-defined project topics or create your own innovative solution by mixing and matching from the categories below.

1. Disease Prediction Model

Develop a model that predicts early onset of chronic diseases using wearable or lifestyle data.

2. EHR Summarization AI

Develop an AI assistant that summarizes electronic health records (EHRs) into concise clinical notes using LLMs.

3. Privacy-Preserving Data Collaboration

Design a privacy-preserving data collaboration system that lets hospitals analyze shared insights using cloud or edge based platforms.

4. IoT Anomaly Detection

Detects anomalies from simulated IoT sensor data to trigger real-time alerts for patients.

5. Drug Interaction Recommender

Build a smart drug interaction recommender that warns of potential adverse combinations using open drug databases.

6. Mental Health Support Chatbot

Create a mental health support chatbot using an open-source or hosted LLM that provides empathetic, ethically safe responses.

7. Multimodal AI Diagnostic

Develop a simple multimodal AI demo that combines medical images and doctor's notes to generate a diagnostic summary.

8. Healthcare Cybersecurity Dashboard

Build a cybersecurity dashboard for healthcare that visualizes simulated attacks and recommends mitigation actions.

9. Hospital Operations Optimizer

Design an AI-powered hospital operations optimizer that predicts bed occupancy, waiting time, or emergency room load.

10. Ethical AI Recommendation System

Design an AI recommendation system for a healthcare-relevant task that includes an ethical/fairness alignment layer.

11. Radiology Workflow Assistant

Design an intelligent agent system that assists radiology data teams in monitoring the quality and consistency of imaging workflows.

12. VR/XR Learning Experience

Design a virtual or extended reality (VR/XR) learning experience integrated with an AI conversational assistant for healthcare education.

Instructions & Deliverables

Project Guidelines

1. Originality & Creativity

Projects must be original and reflect each team's creativity. Solutions should incorporate emerging trends and are encouraged to involve cross-disciplinary collaboration.

2. Topic Selection

Twelve problem statements are available, and each problem may be chosen by a maximum of two teams on a first-come, first-served basis. After selecting a problem, teams must inform the coordinators at the front desk with their team name and project topic immediately to confirm and display their selection.

3. Mentoring Session

A mentoring session will be held during the scheduled time slot. Teams are encouraged to use this opportunity to seek feedback, clarify their approach, and discuss ideas. Mentors will provide general guidance to help teams refine their concepts and stay aligned with the hackathon goals.

4. Independent Work

After the mentoring session, teams may continue working independently from any location of their choice. Teams are responsible for managing their own schedules and ensuring timely submission of their final project.

5. Communication

Participants are encouraged to stay connected through the official Teams channel for any updates, announcements, or discussions during the hackathon.

6. Submission Deadline

The official Google Form for submissions will be available from 11:00 a.m., and all final projects must be submitted by 12:15 p.m.

Required Deliverables

1. GitHub Repository

Each team must submit a GitHub repository containing all code, data, and documentation, including a detailed README that clearly presents the problem statement, team details, datasets, tools, methods, results, and (optionally) challenges or future improvements, along with step-by-step instructions for reproducing the project.

2. Presentation

Each team will have three minutes for their presentation, followed by one to two minutes for Q&A. A live demo may be included as part of the presentation. The three-minute time limit will be strictly enforced. Teams are encouraged to practice their presentations or demos to ensure they are clear, concise, and within the time limit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can participate?

All current UNT students (undergraduate and graduate) are welcome.

How large can teams be?

Teams must be between 3 and 5 students. We will have a team formation event if you don't have one!

How much does it cost?

Participation is completely free. Meals and snacks will be provided.

What if I don't have a team?

We will have a team formation session during the hackathon to help you find teammates with complementary skills.

What should I bring?

Bring your laptop, charger, and any development tools you prefer. We'll provide refreshments and meals.

Who can I contact for more information?

For any questions, please reach out to Pranav at PranavAbishaiMoses@my.unt.edu.
